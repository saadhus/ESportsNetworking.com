What is E-Sports?

So, what really is E-Sports?


‹ ›

E-Sports, or electronic sports, is the competitive play of video games via consoles, desktops, laptops, mobiles or other electronic devices. Fans all around the world gather at tournaments and online to watch their favorite professionals players go head to head.


Garnering an increasingly large audience, E-Sports has evolved from the rare annual competition that it was almost two decades ago to a warmly-embraced part of global culture. Hundreds of thousands of viewers and fans per event or cast is quite common but the fan base is always increasing at an exponential rate.

E-Sports in the News

E-Sports is made up of three main parts...

Gaming Tournaments/Events

Main venues and events where E-Sports takes place


The main venue for E-Sports. Gaming tournaments and events dominate a large part of E-Sports. Large eSports event features thousands of spectators onsite, with hundreds of thousands watching live online. Live broadcasts feature play-by-play commentary, full video production, and are very similar to an ESPN broadcast of a traditional sports game.


Casters and Commentators

Purveyors of E-Sports, also provide content in between events


Professional commentators are what bring E-Sports alive. Found at all major gaming tournaments and events, casters and commentators broadcast live games while commentating on them, very similar to professional commentators of traditional sports. Caster and commentators also cast replays of professional games, which in turn also garners large audiences.


Professional Gamers

Have huge and ever growing fan bases


The actiual players and competiors in E-Sports. Professional Gamers compete in E-Sports events to win prize money and up their standing in the E-Sports community and gain sponsorship. While being dedicated athletes, Pro Gamers all have a huge fan bases behind them, following their every move.
